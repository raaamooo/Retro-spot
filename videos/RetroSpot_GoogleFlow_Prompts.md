## GLOBAL STYLE REFERENCE
> Paste this block at the top of every prompt as a style anchor.

```
GLOBAL STYLE: Black-and-white film base grade, desaturated street world.
Selective color isolation applied ONLY to oil paintings on walls and to
the Retro Spot café interior and signage (warm amber and deep ochre tones).
All other elements remain monochrome. Aspect ratio 9:16 vertical (Instagram Reels).
Cinematic 24fps. Lens: 35mm prime, shallow depth of field.
Audio bed: vinyl crackle + soft jazz for Retro Spot scenes.
Typography on-screen: serif vintage poster style, aged cream color.
```

# PART 1 — THE OLD MAN DISCOVERS RETRO

---

## REEL 1.1 — "A Street in Color"
**Duration target:** 30–45 seconds

---

### SHOT 1 — Wide establishing street

```
[GLOBAL STYLE applied]

Wide shot, eye-level. A narrow urban side street at golden hour, entirely
rendered in black and white. Cobblestone pavement, old building facades.
The street is quiet — one or two blurred pedestrians in the distance.
The walls of the buildings are covered floor-to-ceiling in large
hand-painted murals: birds, geometric patterns, a woman's face, an old
telephone — all isolated in full vivid color against the monochrome world.
The color of the paintings glows as if lit from within.
Camera: slow push-in from the end of the street. 8 seconds.
Audio: faint ambient city sound, then vinyl crackle begins.
```

---

### SHOT 2 — The Old Man enters

```
[CHARACTER A — THE OLD MAN]
[GLOBAL STYLE applied]

Medium-full shot. The Old Man enters frame from the left edge, walking
slowly down the cobblestone street. Black and white world around him.
He pauses. His gaze lifts to the painted murals on the wall — his face
shows quiet wonder, recognition, not surprise. He tilts his head slightly.
He reaches out one gloved hand and stops just short of touching a painting.
Camera: handheld, gentle sway, follows him at shoulder height.
Shallow DOF — street goes soft behind him. 10 seconds.
```

---

### SHOT 3 — He sees Retro Spot

```
[CHARACTER A — THE OLD MAN]
[GLOBAL STYLE applied]

Over-the-shoulder shot from behind the Old Man. His silhouette is
monochrome. In the distance — the Retro Spot storefront erupts in full,
warm color: deep terracotta red signage, warm amber window glow,
brass-trimmed door. It is the only color-saturated object in the entire
frame besides the murals. He turns his head slightly toward it.
Camera: slow zoom in on the storefront over his shoulder, 6 seconds.
Then cut to black.
On-screen text fades in — serif vintage style:
"Some places you don't find. They find you."
```

## REEL 1.2 — "Alo. Retro is back."
**Duration target:** 25–35 seconds

---

### SHOT 1 — The rotary telephone rings

```
[GLOBAL STYLE applied — warm interior color active]

Close-up, low angle. An old black Bakelite rotary telephone sits on
a small wooden table in a dim back room of Retro Spot. The room is
warm — amber wall sconce behind it. The telephone RINGS — the bell
mechanism vibrates visibly. Dust motes in the air.
Camera: static, macro lens on the telephone dial, pull focus to the
room behind it. 5 seconds of ringing.
Audio: rotary telephone bell, then vinyl crackle underscores.
```

---

### SHOT 2 — The Old Man answers

```
[CHARACTER A — THE OLD MAN]
[GLOBAL STYLE applied — warm interior color active]

Medium shot. The Old Man enters the back room — his coat still on,
hat in his hand now. He looks at the telephone without urgency, as
if he expected it. He lifts the receiver slowly and brings it to
his ear. A beat of silence. Then he speaks softly:
DIALOGUE: "Alo."
His eyes are calm, looking at nothing. He listens.
Camera: slow push-in to his face profile. 8 seconds.
```

---

### SHOT 3 — The voice on the line

```
[GLOBAL STYLE applied — warm interior color active]

Extreme close-up: the earpiece of the rotary telephone pressed to
his ear. The crackling audio of a voice comes through:
DIALOGUE (V.O.): "Retro is back."
His eye closes slowly — like hearing something he has waited years for.
Camera: macro on the earpiece, then rack focus to his eye. 5 seconds.
Cut to black. On-screen text:
"Retro is back."
Audio: jazz piano note sustains, then silence.
```

---

### SHOT 1 — The rival café (flashback)

```

Wide shot. A busy, loud modern café — exposed concrete walls, generic
```

### SHOT 2 — She finds Retro on her phone

```
[CHARACTER B — THE GIRL]
[GLOBAL STYLE: still desaturated rival café environment]

the Retro Spot storefront in color against a grey street.
She stops scrolling. She taps. She leans in.
Her face — for the first time — shows a flicker of interest.
Camera: OTS on the phone, then pan up to her face. 6 seconds.
```

---

### SHOT 3 — She arrives at Retro Spot

```
[CHARACTER B — THE GIRL]
[GLOBAL STYLE applied — warm interior color FULLY active]

Medium shot, interior. The Girl pushes open the brass-trimmed door

She exhales. Her shoulders drop. She finds an armchair near a window
and sits — properly sits, for the first time. She closes her eyes
for a single breath.
Camera: slow push-in on her face as she exhales. 10 seconds.
On-screen text:
```

# PRODUCTION NOTES FOR GOOGLE FLOW

## Consistency Prompts Across All Shots

Append these lines to every individual shot prompt to maintain cross-reel consistency:

```
CONSISTENCY NOTES:
- Maintain identical color grade: B&W base, selective warm color
  isolation only on paintings and Retro Spot interior/signage.
- Rival café scenes: complete desaturation, no warm tones anywhere.
- Envelope/letter motion graphic: same hand-drawn illustration style
  across all 5 beats in Reel 2.3. Same looping arc, same ink trail.
- Aspect ratio: 9:16 vertical throughout.
- Do not add lens flares, modern color grading, or HDR effects.
- Grain level: light 35mm film grain on all shots.
```

## Negative Prompts (what to avoid)

```
NEGATIVE PROMPTS (exclude from all generations):
No modern neon signage. No smartphones visible except the Girl's
in Reel 2.1. No contemporary café aesthetics (no exposed Edison bulbs,
no subway tiles, no open-plan industrial look). No fast cutting.
No digital color grading effects. No shaky cam in Retro Spot interior
shots. No direct camera address (no characters look into lens).
No superhero, fantasy, or surreal elements.
```
